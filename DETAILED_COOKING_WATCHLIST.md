## üí° Pro Tips for Using This Watch List

### Watching Strategy:
1. **First watch:** Overview, take notes on ingredients and steps
2. **Second watch:** Pause and prep ingredients alongside the video
3. **Third watch:** Cook along in real-time
4. **Fourth watch:** Review if something went wrong

### Practice Approach:
- **Repeat each recipe 2-3 times** before moving on
- The third time cooking something, you start to internalize it
- **Don't skip the "boring" basics** - they're the foundation

### Learning Habits:
- **Keep a cooking journal:**
  - What you made
  - What worked
  - What didn't
  - What to try differently next time

- **Take photos of your food:**
  - Not for social media
  - For tracking your progress
  - You'll be amazed how much you improve

- **Taste constantly:**
  - Before seasoning
  - After adding salt
  - Before it's "done"
  - Learn what "well-seasoned" tastes like

### Investment Priority:
1. **One good 8-inch chef's knife** ($30-50)
2. **Knife sharpener** ($15-30)
3. **12-inch stainless steel or cast iron skillet** ($30-60)
4. **Medium pot for pasta/rice** ($20-40)
5. **Cutting board** ($15-30)
6. **Basic utensils** (spatula, wooden spoon, tongs) ($20)

**Total starter investment: $130-230**

Don't buy more until you know what you need.

2. **Reddit:** r/Cooking, r/cookingforbeginners
3. **YouTube search:** "[your problem] cooking"
4. **Re-watch the video** - You probably missed something

### Common Beginner Problems & Solutions:

**"Everything burns!"**
- ‚Üí Lower your heat (medium heat is your friend)
- ‚Üí Watch videos on heat control

**"Food is bland"**
- ‚Üí Use more salt (taste as you go)
- ‚Üí Watch seasoning basics videos

**"I don't know when something is done"**
- ‚Üí Use a meat thermometer ($15)
- ‚Üí Watch videos on doneness indicators

- ‚Üí Mise en place (prep everything first)
