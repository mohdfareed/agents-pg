## How to Use This Watch List

### Week-by-Week Approach:
1. **Don't skip stages** - Each builds on the previous
2. **Watch videos multiple times:**
   - First watch: Get the overview
   - Second watch: Take notes
   - Third watch: Cook along with the video
3. **Practice each technique** before moving on
4. **Start a cooking journal:**
   - What worked
   - What didn't
   - Questions to research
   - Recipes you want to try

### Daily Routine Suggestions:
- **Morning:** Watch 1-2 videos while having breakfast/coffee
- **Evening:** Practice what you learned by cooking
- **Weekend:** Tackle longer recipes or techniques

### Key Principles to Remember:
1. **Mistakes are learning opportunities** - Every chef has burned something
2. **Taste as you go** - This is how you learn seasoning
3. **Clean as you cook** - Makes everything more manageable
4. **Keep it simple** - Master basics before getting fancy
5. **Sharp knives are safer** - Invest in a knife sharpener
6. **Mise en place** - Prep everything before you start cooking
7. **High heat isn't always better** - Learn when to use what temperature

## Motivation & Mindset

**Remember:**
- Your parents not teaching you life skills is NOT your fault
- You're taking charge of your life and health - that's amazing!
- Cooking is a life skill that will save you money, improve your health, and bring joy
- Everyone starts somewhere - even Joshua Weissman and Ethan Chlebowski were beginners once
- The cooking community is generally very supportive and helpful
- It's okay to fail - burned food is a rite of passage
- Cooking for yourself is an act of self-care and self-love

**Your Goals:**
- Week 1-2: Have an organized, functional kitchen
- Week 3-4: Feel confident with a knife and basic cooking methods
- Month 2: Cook 3-4 simple meals confidently
- Month 3: Plan and cook a week's worth of meals
- Month 4: Experiment and start developing your own style
- Month 6: Cook confidently without always following recipes

**You've got this!** Cooking is one of the most rewarding skills you can learn. It's creative, practical, and delicious.
